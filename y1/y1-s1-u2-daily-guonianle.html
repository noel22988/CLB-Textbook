Meihua's New Year Posts
Intro. Let's follow Meihua's CNY posts to see how she celebrates Lunar New Year — and learn a few Chinese New Year customs along the way.
贴tiē文wén一yī：大dà扫sǎo除chú
帖tiē文wén农历nóng新lì年要xīn到nián了yào，家dào家le户，户jiā都jiā在hù大hù扫除dōu。我zài们dà用sǎo了chú一yì天tiān的yòng时le间yī把tiān家de里shí打jiān扫bǎ得jiā干lǐ干dǎ净sǎo净dé。大gān扫gān除jìng后jìng，爷dà爷sǎo在chú门hòu口yé贴yé春zài联mén，贴kǒu“福tiē”字。爷chūn爷lián说，，把tiē“福fú”字zì倒yé过yé来shuō贴bǎ，福fú气zì就dào会guò到lái我tiē们fú家qì。
Post 1: Spring Cleaning
Post. Lunar New Year is just around the corner, and every household is busy spring-cleaning. We spent a whole day getting our home spotless. After the cleaning, Grandpa stuck up the Spring Festival couplets at the front door, and the character 福 ("fortune"). Grandpa says that if you stick the 福 upside-down, fortune will come to our home.
贴tiē文wén二èr：买mǎi年货nián
帖tiē文wén今jīn天tiān，我wǒ和hé外wài婆pó去qù了le年货nián市huò场，那shì里chǎng人nà好lǐ多rén啊hǎo！外duō婆a买wài了pó柑mǎi和le年gān糕hé。外nián婆gāo说wài，拜pó年shuō时bài送nián柑shí一yí定dìng要yí送dìng双yào数sòng，意shuāng思shù是yì好si事shì成hǎo双shì。我chéng问shuāng外wǒ婆wèn为wài什pó么wèi过shén年me要guò吃nián年yào糕chī，她nián说gāo，“年tā糕shuō”和nián“年gāo高hé”同nián音gāo，表tóng示yīn年biǎo年shì高nián，生nián活gāo一yì年nián比yī一yì年nián好yī。
Post 2: Buying CNY Goods
Post. Today I went to the CNY goods market with Grandma — it was packed! She bought mandarin oranges and nian gao (sticky rice cake). Grandma said when giving mandarin oranges as a CNY gift, you must give them in pairs — "good things come in twos." I asked her why we eat nian gao at New Year, and she said: 年糕 (nian gao) sounds the same as 年高 ("year high") — it means each year of our lives gets better than the last.
贴tiē文wén三sān：团tuán圆yuán饭fàn
帖tiē文wén每měi年nián过guò年nián，为wèi了le准zhǔn备bèi除夕chú的xī团圆de饭tuán，妈yuán妈fàn都mā把mā冰dōu箱bǎ装bīng得xiāng满zhuāng满dé的mǎn。我mǎn们de的wǒ团men圆de饭tuán一yí定dìng会yí吃dìng鱼huì，因chī为yú“鱼yīn”和wèi“余yú”读hé音yú一yí样yàng，“余yī”有yàng多yú余yǒu的duō意yú思de，吃yì“鱼si”代chī表yú年dài年biǎo有余nián。今nián年yǒu吃yú团。圆jīn饭nián时chī，妹tuán妹yuán不fàn小shí心mèi打mèi碎bù了xiǎo一yí个gè碗dǎ。妈suì妈没le生yí气gè，还wǎn连mā忙mā说méi：“没shēng关qì系hái，没lián关máng系shuō，岁méi岁guān平xì安méi！”
Post 3: The Reunion Dinner
Post. Every year for New Year's Eve, Mum stuffs the fridge full to prepare for the reunion dinner. We always have fish — because 鱼 (fish) sounds the same as 余 ("surplus"), so eating fish means abundance every year. This year at our reunion dinner, my little sister accidentally broke a bowl. Mum didn't get angry — she just quickly said, "No problem, no problem — peace every year!"

1 客kè家jiā人rén是shì新xīn加jiā坡pō华huá族zú中zhōng重zhòng要yào的de方fāng言yán群qún。他tā们men早zǎo期qī在zài新xīn加jiā坡pō从cóng事shì的de行háng业yè各gè有yǒu特tè色sè。
1The Hakka are an important dialect group within Singapore's Chinese community. The trades they took up in early Singapore each had their own distinctive character.
2 一yì些xiē客kè家jiā人rén开kāi当dàng铺pù，让ràng人rén们men典diǎn当dàng物wù品pǐn；一yì些xiē客kè家jiā人rén开kāi眼yǎn镜jìng店diàn，年nián轻qīng人rén就jiù到dào店diàn里lǐ当dāng徒tú弟dì。也yě有yǒu客kè家jiā人rén开kāi药yào材cái铺pù，请qǐng中zhōng医yī师shī看kàn诊zhěn。
2Some Hakka people ran pawn shops, where customers could pawn their belongings; some opened optical shops, where young people came to serve as apprentices. Others opened Chinese medicinal-herb shops, where TCM physicians saw patients.
3 客kè家jiā人rén的de传chuán统tǒng美měi食shí有yǒu算suàn盘pán子zi，因yīn为wèi它tā的de样yàng子zi像xiàng算suàn盘pán的de珠zhū子zi，所suǒ以yǐ叫jiào这zhè个ge名míng字zì。客kè家jiā人rén也yě擅shàn长cháng制zhì作zuò腌yān制zhì类lèi食shí品pǐn，例lì如rú梅méi菜cài、咸xián鱼yú和hé虾xiā米mǐ。
3Among the Hakka's traditional foods is suan pan zi (“abacus beads”) — they get the name because they look like beads on an abacus. The Hakka are also skilled at making pickled and preserved foods — preserved mustard greens, salted fish, and dried shrimp.
4 在zài中zhōng国guó，客kè家jiā人rén的de传chuán统tǒng建jiàn筑zhù——土tǔ楼lóu，是shì大dà家jiā共gòng同tóng居jū住zhù的de圆yuán形xíng大dà房fáng子zi，可kě以yǐ加jiā强qiáng凝níng聚jù力lì。这zhè些xiē都dōu是shì客kè家jiā文wén化huà的de特tè色sè。
4In China, the Hakka's traditional architecture — the tulou — is a large round house where many families live together, strengthening community bonds. All of these are hallmarks of Hakka culture.

《别上假信息的当》 Don't Fall for Fake Information

语速 Speed 0.9× (set before play)
字体 Size

classic example; typical
误wù导dǎo
to mislead; misleading

to identify; to tell apart
1 进jìn入rù信xìn息xī化huà时shí代dài，我wǒ们men每měi天tiān都dū会huì接jiē收shōu大dà量liàng信xìn息xī，其qí中zhōng有yǒu一yì些xiē是shì假jiǎ信xìn息xī。事shì实shí上shàng，假jiǎ信xìn息xī的de传chuán播bō从cóng古gǔ至zhì今jīn都dōu很hěn普pǔ遍biàn，千qiān万wàn不bú要yào小xiǎo看kàn它tā的de影yǐng响xiǎng。
1Now that we live in an information age, we take in huge amounts of information every day — and some of it is fake. The truth is, fake information has been spreading from ancient times right up to today. Don't underestimate the impact it can have.
2 古gǔ代dài的de假jiǎ信xìn息xī大dà多duō以yǐ流liú言yán或huò传chuán闻wén的de形xíng式shì传chuán播bō。汉hàn朝cháo的de刘liú邦bāng在zài领lǐng兵bīng打dǎ仗zhàng时shí，曾céng杀shā死sǐ一yī条tiáo白bái蛇shé。有yǒu传chuán言yán说shuō这zhè条tiáo蛇shé是shì天tiān帝dì的de儿ér子zi，杀shā死sǐ这zhè条tiáo蛇shé意yì味wèi着zhe刘liú邦bāng会huì成chéng为wéi新xīn的de皇huáng帝dì。刘liú邦bāng杀shā蛇shé可kě能néng是shì真zhēn的de，但dàn说shuō杀shā了le蛇shé会huì做zuò皇huáng帝dì就jiù是shì典diǎn型xíng的de假jiǎ信xìn息xī。像xiàng这zhè样yàng的de传chuán言yán在zài不bù同tóng的de朝cháo代dài都dōu有yǒu所suǒ流liú传chuán，都dōu产chǎn生shēng过guò一yí定dìng的de影yǐng响xiǎng。
2In ancient times, fake information mostly travelled in the form of rumour and hearsay. When Liu Bang of the Han dynasty was leading his troops into battle, he was said to have killed a white serpent. A rumour started that the serpent was the son of the Heavenly Emperor — and that killing it meant Liu Bang would become the new emperor. Liu Bang killing a snake may well have been true; but saying that killing it would make him emperor is a classic example of fake information. Rumours like this circulated through every dynasty, and each carried some real impact.
3 今jīn天tiān，假jiǎ信xìn息xī的de数shù量liàng和hé传chuán播bō速sù度dù都dōu远yuǎn远yuǎn超chāo过guò古gǔ代dài，影yǐng响xiǎng也yě更gèng大dà。本běn世shì纪jì初chū，互hù联lián网wǎng上shàng就jiù曾céng出chū现xiàn“2012年nián世shì界jiè末mò日rì将jiāng会huì降jiàng临lín”的de流liú言yán。许xǔ多duō人rén信xìn以yǐ为wéi真zhēn，给gěi自zì己jǐ的de生shēng活huó带dài来lái了le影yǐng响xiǎng。
3Today, fake information far surpasses the past in both volume and speed of spread, and its impact is greater too. At the start of this century, a rumour spread on the internet that "the world will end in 2012." Many people took it as truth, and it affected their lives.
4 假jiǎ信xìn息xī有yǒu很hěn大dà的de误wù导dǎo性xìng，会huì让ràng人rén把bǎ假jiǎ的dí当dàng成chéng真zhēn的de。有yǒu一yí个gè成chéng语yǔ“三sān人rén成chéng虎hǔ”，来lái自zì这zhè样yàng一yí个gè故gù事shì。古gǔ代dài有yǒu人rén曾céng这zhè样yàng提tí醒xǐng魏wèi王wáng，如rú果guǒ只zhǐ有yǒu一yí个gè人rén对duì魏wèi王wáng说shuō街jiē上shàng出chū现xiàn老lǎo虎hǔ，他tā一yí定dìng觉jué得de不bù可kě能néng，也yě不bú会huì信xìn。但dàn如rú果guǒ很hěn多duō人rén都dōu跟gēn他tā这zhè样yàng说shuō，他tā就jiù会huì信xìn以yǐ为wéi真zhēn。这zhè个ge故gù事shì告gào诉sù我wǒ们men，假jiǎ信xìn息xī重chóng复fù的de次cì数shù多duō了le，很hěn容róng易yì使shǐ人rén分fēn不bù清qīng真zhēn假jiǎ。
4Fake information is highly misleading — it can make people take what is false as true. There's an idiom: “three men make a tiger” (三人成虎), and it comes from this story. Long ago, someone advised the King of Wei: if only one person told the king there was a tiger in the street, he'd think it was impossible and wouldn't believe it. But if many people kept telling him the same thing, he'd come to believe it as fact. The story teaches us: when fake information is repeated often enough, it becomes very easy to lose track of what's real.
5 我wǒ们men要yào提tí高gāo识shí别bié假jiǎ信xìn息xī的de能néng力lì，学xué会huì独dú立lì思sī考kǎo，这zhè样yàng才cái不bú会huì轻qīng易yì受shòu到dào假jiǎ信xìn息xī的de影yǐng响xiǎng。
5We need to improve our ability to spot fake information, and learn to think for ourselves — only then will we not be easily swayed by it.

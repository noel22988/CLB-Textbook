1 雨yǔ树shù，我wǒ们men的de树shù，我wǒ们men无wú处chǔ不bù在zài的de风fēng景jǐng。
1The rain tree — our tree. Our landscape, wherever we go.
2 每měi天tiān早zǎo上shàng，太tài阳yáng出chū来lái后hòu，她tā把bǎ休xiū息xī了le一yí夜yè的de叶yè子zi一yī一yī展zhǎn开kāi，然rán后hòu在zài蓝lán天tiān下xià站zhàn出chū一yí路lù风fēng景jǐng。每měi天tiān傍bàng晚wǎn，不bù知zhī是shì不bú是shì累lèi了le，她tā会huì把bǎ翠cuì绿lǜ的de叶yè子zi轻qīng轻qīng合hé上shàng。不bù管guǎn什shén么me时shí候hòu，当dāng你nǐ从cóng远yuǎn处chù看kàn她tā，她tā都dōu像xiàng一yī把bǎ大dà伞sǎn一yí样yàng，无wú私sī地dì为wéi人rén们men遮zhē阳yáng挡dǎng雨yǔ。
2Each morning, after the sun rises, she unfolds her leaves one by one after a night's rest, and stands beneath the blue sky to make a stretch of scenery. Each evening — perhaps weary — she gently folds her emerald leaves shut. Whenever you look at her from afar, she is just like a great umbrella, selflessly shading people from sun and rain.
3 雨yǔ树shù，是shì新xīn加jiā坡pō无wú处chǔ不bù在zài的de风fēng景jǐng。不bù论lùn是shì在zài停tíng车chē场chǎng里lǐ、草cǎo场chǎng边biān、还hái是shì在zài道dào路lù的de两liǎng旁páng，我wǒ们men都dōu可kě以yǐ发fā现xiàn雨yǔ树shù的de踪zōng影yǐng。如rú果guǒ你nǐ住zhù的de地dì方fāng比bǐ雨yǔ树shù高gāo，那nà么me，当dāng她tā开kāi花huā时shí，你nǐ可kě以yǐ看kàn到dào大dà片piàn粉fěn红hóng的de花huā海hǎi。在zài清qīng风fēng中zhōng，花huā儿ér轻qīng轻qīng地de摇yáo摆bǎi，叶yè子zi发fā出chū沙shā沙shā的de声shēng响xiǎng，令lìng人rén心xīn情qíng愉yú快kuài。
3The rain tree is a landscape woven through every corner of Singapore. In car parks, at the edge of playing fields, lining the roads — wherever you go, you'll find traces of her. If you live higher up than the rain tree, then when she's in bloom, you'll see a great pink sea of blossoms. In the gentle breeze, the flowers sway lightly and the leaves rustle — it lifts the spirit.
4 有yǒu时shí，我wǒ站zhàn在zài雨yǔ树shù下xià，欣xīn赏shǎng她tā美měi丽lì的de姿zī态tài。看kàn着zhe，看kàn着zhe，她tā好hǎo像xiàng变biàn成chéng了le一yí个gè舞wǔ蹈dǎo员yuán。她tā的de枝zhī干gàn看kàn似shì随suí意yì生shēng长zhǎng，却què可kě以yǐ长zhǎng出chū一yì些xiē非fēi常cháng特tè别bié的de姿zī势shì，像xiàng孩hái子zi一yí样yàng跟gēn鸟niǎo儿ér玩wán捉zhuō迷mí藏cáng。她tā褐hè色sè的de根gēn也yě常cháng常cháng从cóng泥ní土tǔ里lǐ冒mào出chū来lái，向xiàng四sì处chù伸shēn展zhǎn。
4Sometimes I stand beneath her, admiring her lovely form. Look long enough, and she seems to become a dancer. Her branches look as if they grow every which way, but they form the most particular poses — like a child playing hide-and-seek with the birds. Her brown roots, too, often poke up out of the soil and stretch out in all directions.
5 雨yǔ树shù，你nǐ姿zī态tài万wàn千qiān，优yōu美měi动dòng人rén，你nǐ是shì我wǒ们men的de树shù，我wǒ们men无wú处chǔ不bù在zài的de风fēng景jǐng！
5Rain tree — you of the thousand poses, graceful and moving, you are our tree, our landscape, wherever we go!

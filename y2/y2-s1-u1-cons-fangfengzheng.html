《放风筝》 Flying a Kite

语速 Speed 0.9× (set before play)
字体 Size

生词 Vocabulary · 3 words

风fēng筝zheng
kite
挣zhèng脱tuō
to break free; to wrench oneself loose
解jiě释shì
to explain; explanation
1 星xīng期qī二èr晚wǎn上shàng，我wǒ很hěn晚wǎn才cái回huí到dào家jiā。一yī进jìn门mén，我wǒ就jiù看kàn见jiàn妈mā妈mā皱zhòu着zhe眉méi头tóu坐zuò在zài沙shā发fā上shàng。妈mā妈mā不bù高gāo兴xìng地dì说shuō：“为wèi什shén么me这zhè么me晚wǎn回huí家jiā？”她tā不bù听tīng我wǒ解jiě释shì，就jiù说shuō了le我wǒ一yī顿dùn。我wǒ感gǎn到dào很hěn委wěi屈qu，快kuài步bù走zǒu进jìn房fáng间jiān，重chóng重chóng地dì把bǎ门mén关guān上shàng。
1Tuesday evening, I got home very late. The moment I walked in, I saw Mum sitting on the sofa with her brows furrowed. She said unhappily, "Why are you home so late?" She didn't listen to my explanation, and just gave me an earful. I felt so wronged. I walked quickly to my room and slammed the door shut.
2 接jiē下xià来lái的de几jǐ天tiān，我wǒ都dōu不bù跟gēn妈mā妈mā说shuō话huà。
2For the next few days, I didn't speak to Mum at all.
3 星xīng期qī天tiān早zǎo上shàng，爸bà爸bà说shuō全quán家jiā一yì起qǐ去qù放fàng风fēng筝zheng。我wǒ心xīn想xiǎng：我wǒ还hái没méi和hé妈mā妈mā和hé好hǎo，她tā怎zěn么me也yě要yào去qù？
3Sunday morning, Dad said the whole family was going to fly a kite. I thought: I haven't made up with Mum yet — why is she coming too?
4 来lái到dào海hǎi边biān，妈mā妈mā微wēi笑xiào着zhe把bǎ风fēng筝zheng线xiàn放fàng到dào我wǒ手shǒu里lǐ。在zài她tā的de帮bāng助zhù下xià，风fēng筝zheng飞fēi上shàng了le天tiān。
4At the beach, Mum smiled and placed the kite string into my hand. With her help, the kite rose into the sky.
5 突tū然rán，一yí阵zhèn风fēng吹chuī来lái，风fēng筝zheng在zài空kōng中zhōng上shàng下xià摇yáo摆bǎi。我wǒ连lián忙máng把bǎ风fēng筝zheng线xiàn往wǎng回huí拉lā，可kě是shì风fēng筝zheng却què像xiàng个gè不bù听tīng话huà的de孩hái子zi，用yòng力lì挣zhèng脱tuō。线xiàn断duàn了le，风fēng筝zheng落luò在zài矮ǎi树shù丛cóng中zhōng。
5Suddenly, a gust of wind came, and the kite started bobbing wildly in the air. I quickly pulled the string in — but the kite, like a disobedient child, yanked itself free. The string snapped, and the kite came down in a patch of low bushes.
6 妈mā妈mā赶gǎn紧jǐn过guò去qù帮bāng我wǒ捡jiǎn风fēng筝zheng。她tā弯wān下xià身shēn子zi解jiě着zháo风fēng筝zheng线xiàn，额é头tóu上shàng都dōu是shì豆dòu大dà的de汗hàn珠zhū。爸bà爸bà对duì我wǒ说shuō：“你nǐ想xiǎng想xiǎng，自zì己jǐ像xiàng不bù像xiàng风fēng筝zheng？风fēng筝zheng线xiàn握wò在zài他tā人rén手shǒu里lǐ，风fēng筝zheng总zǒng想xiǎng挣zhèng脱tuō；挣zhèng脱tuō后hòu，却què再zài也yě无wú法fǎ高gāo飞fēi了le！”
6Mum hurried over to retrieve the kite for me. She bent down to untangle the string, beads of sweat as big as soybeans on her forehead. Dad said to me: "Think about it — aren't you a bit like the kite? The string is in someone else's hands, and the kite always wants to break loose. But once it does break loose — it can never fly high again."
7 爸bà爸bà的de话huà让ràng我wǒ恍huǎng然rán大dà悟wù。原yuán来lái孩hái子zi可kě以yǐ享xiǎng受shòu自zì由yóu，是shì因yīn为wèi有yǒu父fù母mǔ在zài身shēn旁páng守shǒu护hù着zhe。
7Dad's words made it suddenly clear to me. Children can enjoy freedom precisely because their parents are right there, watching over them.
